LOST REALM

Green unfurls out from beneath your feet and down, down a gentle hill. Along its perimeter emerges a wall of trees standing sentinel, their forms dark but not imposing.

Their presence draws boundaries around the world you know, a gentle but firm reminder: “This is what there is. You are welcome to explore here. Everything beyond is for a later time.” The adult you who observes this place might find their presence claustrophobic, but you have long since slipped out of time.

Now, that boundary feels comforting.


There is no need to be concerned with what lies outside just yet — all in due time.
